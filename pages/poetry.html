This is my poetry.

indiana dunes

we read poems
about lake
michigan. two
had death in
them. we read
sharmel iris,
silver moons
and silver ships

and all that
silver meant
something, all
that silver
held a forgotten
beauty i felt
we couldn't
understand.

it was there i
think but we
could only
ask why so
much silver?

loss

now you are
gone
and the ribbon
you fluttered
through spacetime
will fray

and I pray
that some strand
accounts for
your being

that you may
revisit your
favorite folds

(for grandma)

time

idle more
if you can

melt your
glass shard
thoughts
and make fear
not gone
but empty

until the hums
and sizzles
of your gut
heart
toes
etcetera

wake you to their
vague organic
doings

winter

walking
opposite me
through the
quad
starring at
the tip-tops
of trees
leafless as
they are in
december

with a gentle
expression
not a smile
better maybe
but what
does it matter

the sun
showed pink
in her
cheeks

spring

rabbits in
the yard
again
not the same
as last year
i'd imagine

though
i suppose
i have never known
the movements
and patterns
of those creatures

headstone

the cemetery
is a narrow
strip of land

between a house
and a field
of wild grass

time moves
backwards
when we walk
to the far end

all the way
to a pair of
civil war
veterans

ancestors
probably

and i wish i
didn't find this
so fascinating

where is
my
solemnity?

grandpa says
the marker is
nice
the way
one speaks of
an apartment

what do i
do with
a picture
of me and
a headstone?

god, what a
pleasant day
it is

this will
all be mine
one day
or ours
or no one's

or maybe
this will
all be me
one day
or us
or no one

my cousin
asks if i'm okay
i've been staring
at the grave
and the flat
midwestern sky

i hesitate,
i want to
say that i feel

that the
event
has reached
a limit
point

that i'm almost
giddy with
the prospect of
nothingness

that i'm not thinking
of grandma in
this moment
only of death
and, god,
what a pleasant
day it is

night

you cried
on the balcony
of that berkeley
apartment above
the tattoo parlor

i held you
from behind
and admired the
streetlight haze

and i lingered
at the periphery
of this
experience
like an idiot

an observer
of your
loneliness
content with
my assumed
tenderness

i was afraid
to take on
your trauma
then

and we never
spoke again
about it
in the years
that followed

crush

i hate that
she smokes

take my lungs
lead and arsenic

(an aspirational
sentiment
at best)

she asked me
to read
the buddha in
the attic
an assignment
from her
asian studies
course

which really
did mean
nothing

we met for
breakfast
to discuss it
at a place
that was
all windows
and long tables

and here i
performed
for her

the role of
pretend polymath
(a favorite
of mine)

in a scene
saturated by
her quiet
glow

now all i can
recollect
is quantized

sunlight on
dark wood

her serious
countenance

her tortoise
shell glasses
